## Structural Notes

**Episodes published as full drops (under ~3,500 words):**
Episodes 1, 2, 3, 4, 9, 10, 11, 12, 14, 15, 16 — and the 17+18 finale pairing.

**Episodes split into two parts (over ~3,500 words):**
Episodes 5, 6, 7, 8, 13. Each split targets ~1,750–2,500 words per part. Split at natural scene breaks — paragraph gaps, tonal shifts, or moments of tension that make a reader want to come back.

**Finale pairing (Week 22):**
The Stick (Jessica, ~1,440 words) and The Knock (Elijah, ~1,080 words) publish together as a single ~2,520-word finale. The Stick is the POV shift — suddenly we're seeing from outside the marriage. The Knock is the gut punch. Publishing them together gives readers the full one-two ending without a week of waiting between the setup and the hit.

## Rhythm and Pacing Notes

**Weeks 1–3 (Free):** Camille → Elijah → Camille. Establishes both voices. Reader gets the devastation from both sides. By end of Week 3, they're hooked and they've seen both the architect's precision and the financier's composure crack. Paywall is natural — you want to keep reading.

**Weeks 4–12 (The Middle):** The longest stretch. The reconciliation arc. Two-part episodes slow the pace, which matches the narrative — this is the slow, uncertain rebuilding. Readers are living inside the tension of "will they or won't they" week by week.

**Weeks 13–16 (The Turn):** Kevin enters. Full episodes again — the pace picks up. His and The Breathing are the shortest drops in the series. Short hits. Punchy. The story is accelerating.

**Weeks 17–22 (The Endgame):** Parking Lot through the finale. Restoration in two parts (the sex, then the aftermath). The Montage gives readers the relief they've earned. Then the floor drops: The Stick. The Knock. Done.

## Tiered Distribution Strategy: Substack vs. Website

Substack serialization is the wide-distribution play — discoverability, subscriber growth, algorithmic reach. But direct website subscribers (powerhousenovelas.com) get a premium experience. This creates a genuine value differential and a reason to subscribe direct.

**PHN Substack ($10/month — all PHN content):**
- One subscription covers everything PHN publishes: Broken Vase, After the Silence, future series, essays, standalone pieces
- Text-only experience
- Once per week per series (multiple series may run concurrently)
- Episodes 1–3 of each series free, paywall after
- Broken Vase is the launch serialization; other series will layer in on their own schedules

**PHN Website (Premium — price TBD, higher than Substack):**
- Same catalog, different experience
- Full immersive delivery: music, visuals, audio narration, the complete audiovisual experience
- Options for differentiation (pick one or combine):
  1. **Bigger delivery units.** Website subscribers get full episodes regardless of length. When Substack gets The Morning Part 1, website gets the whole Morning (~3,500 words). Always ahead of the split.
  2. **Early access.** Website subscribers get each drop 48–72 hours before Substack.
  3. **Bundled delivery.** Website subscribers receive 3–4 episodes at a time instead of weekly singles.
  4. **Full book on completion.** Website subscribers get the complete epub/PDF the moment the finale publishes on Substack.
  5. **Additives exclusive to website.** Music, visuals, audio — the full sensory experience lives on the website only. Substack gets text. The website gets the world.

The clearest moat is **additives exclusivity**. Substack is the words. The website is the world. That justifies the price differential without punishing Substack subscribers — they get everything that matters for reading. Website subscribers get everything that matters for *experiencing*.
