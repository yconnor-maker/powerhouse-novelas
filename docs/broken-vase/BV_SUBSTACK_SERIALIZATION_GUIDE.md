## SERIES DESCRIPTION
### For Substack Series Page (~200 words)

**Broken Vase** is a literary novel about desire, marriage, and the woman who walks away—then back—then stops walking altogether.

After her husband's affair with her closest cousin destroys her marriage, Camille is left holding the pieces: her three children, her architect's hands that have been idle for five years, and the knowledge that she chose this life. What nobody prepared her for is that the man her body still recognizes—the man who broke her—might be worth the risk of rebuilding.

This is not a love story. This is not a redemption arc. This is what happens when two people, broken open, decide to find out what happens next.

Told across eighteen episodes from three points of view—Camille's architectural precision, Elijah's financial logic, Jessica's fearless clarity—*Broken Vase* traces the architecture of desire and the cost of choosing yourself, again and again, in a marriage that won't let you go. The narrative moves through the body before the mind has time to refuse it: raw, intelligent, sexually explicit, and deeply literary.

*Broken Vase* is literary fiction that happens to include sex. Not romance. Not erotica. A novel for readers who expect their fiction to land in their bodies and linger in their heads.

**PHN Tagline:** Power. Medicine. Desire.

## EPISODE HOOKS
### Substack Post Headers (1–2 sentences, literary + provocative)

**Episode 1: The Visit**
She walks back into their house like she's never left, and Elijah pretends not to notice that she has.

**Episode 2: After the Visit**
He lies awake with her in the house and learns that proximity isn't the same as forgiveness—but it's a place to start.

**Episode 3: The Cracks**
The house remembers her. She's not sure she remembers herself. A woman returns to the place she left, searching for the architecture of what she used to be.

**Episode 4: The Remainder**
He has spent five years in the shape her absence made. Now she's standing in his house, unmeasuring it with her presence alone.

**Episode 5: The Morning**
First light. His hand reaches across the bed before his mind catches up, before memory can tell him this isn't real.

**Episode 6: Staying**
To stay with someone is not the same as loving them. It is harder. It is more honest. It requires permission to break.

**Episode 7: The Bubble**
Time folds when he touches her—the world becomes optional. She has five years of starvation that his hands can answer.

**Episode 8: The Bid**
He makes an offer. She has to decide if she's the kind of woman who accepts it, or the kind of woman who asks for more.

**Episode 9: The Cost**
Every choice has a price in this marriage. She counts it in the dark, alone, deciding whether she can afford to stay.

**Episode 10: The Third Option**
Her career ended five years ago when she chose domesticity. Now a conversation with her husband opens a door she thought was sealed forever.

**Episode 11: The Breathing**
He learns to breathe differently when she's in the room. Fear and desire have the same lung capacity. Both require practice.

**Episode 12: His**
She has never belonged to anyone—not fully. Now, in a dark bedroom, she finds out what that costs. And whether she'll pay it.

**Episode 13: The Parking Lot**
A text from Jessica arrives. One decision unmade in a parking lot cascade into a call that cannot be unmade.

**Episode 14: Restoration**
In the bedroom, they rebuild what breaking made. It is more honest than anything before, more intimate, more true.

**Episode 15: Restoration Part Two: The Middle of the Night**
3 AM. The body knows things the mind refuses to admit. They have always communicated best in the dark.

**Episode 16: The Montage**
Small moments accrete across weeks. A touch. A conversation. A quiet decision to build something neither of them planned.

**Episode 17: The Stick**
She holds the test in her hand. The positive line changes nothing and everything. The future has just become irreversible.

**Episode 18: The Knock**
A knock at the door. The person on the other side of it changes everything they've rebuilt. Everything unmeasures again.

## VOICE & TONE REFERENCE
### Editorial Guidelines for All Marketing Copy

**Monica Crayon's voice:**
- Clinical, direct, warm underneath
- No sugar. No apologies.
- Confidence in the literary quality
- Acknowledge the sexual content without sensationalizing it
- Position the work ABOVE genre fiction
- Use architectural / structural metaphors when writing about the narrative
- Short sentences mixed with longer ones (don't over-explain)

**Powerhouse Novelas brand voice:**
- "Fiction enters the body before the mind has time to refuse it."
- Prestige-TV quality
- Intellectual + sensual (not one or the other)
- Refuse genre categorization; respect it; transcend it
- Leadership in literary erotic fiction, not commercial erotica

**Do NOT use:**
- Romance-genre language ("swoon," "steamy," "heart-pounding," etc.)
- Euphemisms for sex or bodies
- Soft or diminishing language
- False modesty or over-explanation
- Genre-typical framing ("a romance for adults who...")

**DO use:**
- Specific language about what the book IS
- Confidence in literary positioning
- Architectural/structural language
- Direct address to the reader's intelligence
- Intellectual credibility alongside erotic content
