Power
Moves
Through
Bodies

Serialized fiction for readers who want more than plot. A body of work organized as an ecosystem — its coherence comes from the consistent examination of power, desire, responsibility, and consequence.

The work is erotically explicit because distance distorts truth. It does not privilege heterosexuality as a default. It does not moralize desire. It asks: how does power actually move through intimate relationships — and what happens when someone refuses to be managed?

Every story in this universe shares a common architecture: the body reveals what language conceals. Power — not love, not morality, not individual goodness — is the organizing force.

Part of the Monica Craiyon universe.

The Architecture

The Rules of This World

Principle I

Power Is the Organizing Force

Not love. Not morality. Power — how it moves, who holds it, who refuses it — is the architecture that governs every relationship and body in this universe.

Principle II

The Body Does Not Lie

The body reveals what language conceals. Desire, exhaustion, violence, pleasure — these are data points, not metaphors.

Principle III

Distance Distorts Truth

Pulling back from the body creates distance that allows the reader to remain comfortable rather than implicated.

Principle IV

No Default Hierarchies

This universe does not privilege heterosexuality as a default. It does not moralize desire. It observes power dynamics in all configurations.

Principle V

Fiction as Truth Delivery

Fiction enters the body before the mind has time to refuse it. Story bypasses the defenses that keep people from seeing what they already know.

Principle VI

Ecosystem, Not Catalog

Every series is connected — not by plot, but by architecture. The same examination runs through every story.
