Philosophy & Architecture

The World We Build

Powerhouse Novelas operates from a narrative assumption that some readers will find uncomfortable: asymmetrical or mixed attraction is far more common than public identity suggests. Power — not love, not morality, not individual goodness — is the organizing force of every intimacy and every institution.

This is a collaborative creation by a diverse team of writers. We deliberately distance our characters from our personal lives, challenging the common misconception that fiction written by women forms a confessional diary. We do not write autobiographies masked as narratives; we map the systemic forces that shape ambition, desire, and survival across clinical and emotional environments.

We do not privilege heterosexuality as the default lens. We do not write around the body; we write exclusively through it. The body is the first and final arbiter of truth, bypassing the intellectual justifications we construct to stay comfortable.

The Architecture of Asymmetry

We examine how power distributes itself unequally in every relationship. We explore the dynamics of betrayal, the silent agreements that uphold open and closed structures, and why the truth is often weaponized before it is acknowledged.

Emotional Cartography

The terrain where desire and betrayal converge. Our stories dissect the fault lines in clinical environments where the consequences of desire carry both professional and intensely personal weight.

Systemic Friction

Characters who refuse to be managed will inevitably collide with the institutions designed to contain them. Be it a hospital hierarchy or a marital expectation, we chart the destruction—and rebirth—that follows such collisions.
